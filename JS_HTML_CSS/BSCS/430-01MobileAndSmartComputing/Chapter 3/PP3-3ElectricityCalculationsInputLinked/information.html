The Relationship of Electricity's Elements

Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law states that the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage applied across it and inversely proportional to the resistance of the conductor.

Mathematically, it can be expressed as:
i = v / r
    Where:
  • i represents the current flowing through the conductor (in amperes, A ).
  • v represents the voltage across the conductor (in volts, V ).
  • r represents the impedance, or resistance, of the conductor (in ohms, 立 ).

According to Ohm's Law, if the voltage remains constant and the resistance increases, the current flowing through the circuit decreases.
Conversely, if the resistance remains constant and the voltage increases, the current flowing through the circuit increases.
The amount of electricity consumed in a circuit can be determined by the power equation:

p = i * v
    Where:
  • p relates the power consumed (measured in watts, W ) to i, and v.

This equation shows the direct proportionality between p, i and v.

The Relationship Demonstrated Through Speaker Operation

When an audio signal is applied to a speaker, it generates an alternating current (AC) that flows through the voice coil (the speaker's impedance).
The electricity a speaker consumes is directly related to the power it dissipates, which is determined by the voltage across the speaker and the current flowing through it.

These formulas can be used to find the required i and v for a specific speaker according it its specification:
p = i 2 * r
p = v 2 / r
